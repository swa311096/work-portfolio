# Ad Objectives

Ad objectives are the bridge between business goals and delivery logic.

An advertiser is not just telling the platform "show my ad."

They are usually telling the platform something closer to:

- help me reach more people
- send traffic to my site
- get app installs
- generate leads
- drive purchases

That choice matters because it changes what the system tries to optimize for.

## Why Objectives Matter

The same creative can perform differently depending on the objective behind it.

A campaign optimized for:

- reach
- click-through
- video views
- purchases

is not really the same campaign, even if the ad itself looks identical.

The platform may change:

- who gets prioritized in delivery
- what signals matter most
- what the model predicts
- how pacing behaves
- what counts as success in reporting

## Common Objective Buckets

Most platforms expose some version of these buckets:

- awareness or reach
- traffic
- engagement or video views
- app installs
- leads
- conversions or purchases

Different naming systems exist, but the logic is similar.

## The Core Tradeoff

Objective choice is also a tradeoff choice.

For example:

- reach objectives maximize coverage, not efficiency
- traffic objectives can generate cheap clicks that do not convert
- conversion objectives are stronger when event feedback is clean
- purchase objectives can struggle if volume is too low for the model to learn

So the question is not just:

**What outcome do I want?**

It is also:

**Do I have the signal quality and volume needed for the platform to optimize toward that outcome well?**

## Why This Matters In A Company Teardown

When I look at an ads platform, I want to understand:

- what objective menu the platform exposes
- which objectives are likely strongest in practice
- where the platform has enough feedback to optimize reliably
- which objectives create the biggest measurement or trust problems

That usually says a lot about the maturity of the ads system.
