# Education

The business of learning. This section captures how I think about learning outcomes, motivation, instructor economics, curriculum design, and education business models.

## What I'm Trying To Build

My goal here is to build a working understanding of education so I can:

1. explain what makes an education product actually work for learners
2. deconstruct an EdTech company's model across learner value, pedagogy, operations, and economics
3. reason about where most online education products fail and why
4. think clearly about tradeoffs between learning outcomes, instructor quality, scale, and profitability
5. back that up with structured notes, frameworks, and opportunity memos

## What That Means In Practice

There are four capabilities I want this section to demonstrate.

### 1. Explain An Education Business Clearly

For any education business, I want to be able to describe:

- who the learner is
- what outcome they are paying for
- what the learning experience is designed to achieve
- where motivation breaks
- how instruction is delivered
- how the company makes money
- what has to be true for the model to scale

### 2. Deconstruct A Specific Education Product

For companies like Coursera, Duolingo, MasterClass, Interview Kickstart, Byju's, Udemy, or university-adjacent programs, I want to be able to answer:

- what learner need is being served
- what the pedagogy actually is
- what role instructors play
- how curriculum quality is maintained
- how learner accountability is created
- what the economics of acquisition, delivery, and support look like
- what the model's biggest failure modes are
- what the next product opportunities could be

### 3. Talk Like A PM Or Operator In Education

I want to be comfortable reasoning through questions like:

- why do many learners enroll but fail to complete?
- when does live instruction justify its cost?
- what breaks when a course business scales too quickly?
- how should curriculum and instructor quality be measured?
- what makes cohort-based models different from self-serve content libraries?
- where do motivation systems help, and where do they become cosmetic?

### 4. Show Evidence, Not Just Beliefs

The point is to demonstrate a coherent understanding of learning design and education economics, not just opinions about what education should look like.

Every bucket should produce a concrete output artifact.

## What I Want To Produce

This section is designed to produce:

### 1. Education Knowledge Map

A master reference for:

- learning model archetypes
- pedagogy concepts
- instructor and delivery models
- learner success metrics
- pricing and business model comparisons

### 2. Company Teardown Briefs

For target companies, I want short notes covering:

- how the product works
- what learning outcome it promises
- what the instructional model is
- how learner accountability is created
- what the economics look like
- where the model is strong
- where it is fragile

### 3. Product Opportunity Memos

Short PM-style notes such as:

- how a cohort course can improve completion without adding unsustainable ops load
- how a self-paced platform can create real learner accountability
- how instructor utilization can improve without hurting learner quality

### 4. Metrics And System Walkthroughs

Short artifacts on problems like:

- diagnosing completion drop-offs
- measuring learning quality beyond satisfaction scores
- balancing instructor utilization with learner outcomes
- evaluating whether a new support intervention is worth the cost

### 5. Portfolio-Style Interview Stories

I also want to translate real work into education-language stories:

- curriculum launches
- instructor onboarding and training
- instructor cost optimization
- sales funnel redesign for learner conversion
- operational quality improvements
