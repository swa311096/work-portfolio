# Ads & Monetization

How ad platforms actually work — from UX surfaces and auction mechanics to delivery, measurement, and infrastructure. The goal is not just to "understand ads," but to build the kind of domain fluency, company fluency, and strategic judgment that holds up in interviews and hiring-manager conversations.

## Final Goal

By the end, you should be able to walk into an interview or hiring-manager chat and credibly say:

1. I understand the ads ecosystem deeply.
2. I understand how your company's ads model works specifically.
3. I understand the user experience, auction, delivery, measurement, and infrastructure tradeoffs.
4. I can identify the next value-creation opportunities.
5. I have structured artifacts that prove this understanding.

## What Good Looks Like

By the end, you should be able to do four things well.

### 1. Explain Any Ads Business Clearly

You should be able to describe:

- how the company makes money from ads
- who the key actors are
- what the advertiser objective mix looks like
- where monetization happens in the user journey
- how auction, relevance, and measurement likely work at a high level

### 2. Deconstruct A Specific Company's Ads System

For companies like TikTok, Amazon, Google, Disney, Netflix, Meta, or Apple Search Ads, you should be able to answer:

- what intent users come with
- where ads fit in the product experience
- what the advertiser value proposition is
- what the ranking problem is
- what the measurement challenge is
- what the monetization constraints are
- what the moat is
- what the next 2-3 ad product opportunities could be

### 3. Talk Like A PM Or Technical PM In Ads

You should be comfortable reasoning through questions like:

- if revenue is down, where would you look?
- if advertiser ROI is falling, what are the likely causes?
- if ad load increases, what breaks first?
- how search ads and feed ads differ fundamentally
- why commerce ads have a different flywheel
- why brand ads create different UX and measurement challenges
- how privacy changes affect delivery and attribution

### 4. Show Evidence, Not Just Claim Understanding

The differentiator is not saying you understand ads. The differentiator is having structured proof that you do.

## The Three Learning Layers

### Layer A. Core Understanding

This is the knowledge layer:

- ecosystem
- formats
- UX integration
- auction and delivery
- targeting and ML
- tracking and experimentation
- infrastructure, fraud, and privacy
- company-specific monetization models

### Layer B. Applied Company Understanding

This is the company deconstruction layer. For each important company, you should be able to articulate:

- business model
- ad surfaces
- user journey integration
- likely ranking logic
- measurement model
- tradeoffs
- future opportunities

### Layer C. Evidence Creation

This is the differentiator. Every bucket should produce an output artifact that proves real understanding.

## Evidence Stack

By the end, you should have five kinds of artifacts.

### 1. Ads Knowledge Map

A structured master document that captures:

- major concepts
- definitions
- key formulas and metrics
- system diagrams
- platform archetypes
- company comparisons

### 2. Company Teardown Briefs

For each target company, create a concise 2-4 page note covering:

- how ads work there
- user experience integration
- advertiser value proposition
- likely auction and delivery logic
- measurement stack
- major tradeoffs
- whitespace and next opportunities
- interview-ready talking points

### 3. Product Opportunity Memos

Short PM-style notes such as:

- 3 ad opportunities for TikTok brand innovation
- how Disney can improve ad relevance without hurting premium UX
- how Amazon can improve sponsored placement quality for mid-funnel discovery
- how Netflix can evolve brand ad measurement beyond completion rate

### 4. System Design And Metrics Walkthroughs

Short artifacts on problems like:

- design ad ranking for short-form video
- diagnose a CTR drop
- improve ad delivery with sparse conversion data
- balance monetization against user retention
- measure success of a new ad format

### 5. Portfolio-Style Interview Stories

Translate adjacent experience into strong ads-language stories, such as:

- ad creator platforms
- ad tooling at scale
- monetization growth
- feed relevance and engagement changes
- distribution systems
- SEO plus monetization
- experiment design
- revenue versus UX tradeoffs

## Curriculum Design Principle

Every bucket should include three things:

- concepts: what this topic is
- company examples: how it shows up differently across platforms
- output artifact: what you will produce as proof

That keeps the curriculum anchored to actual outcomes rather than passive reading.

## End-State Proof

The end goal is to be able to say:

> I've built a structured understanding of ads across auction, delivery, UX integration, measurement, infrastructure, and platform-specific monetization. I've also done comparative teardowns of companies like TikTok, Amazon, Google, and streaming platforms, and developed product opportunity memos for where value can be created next.

And to back that up with evidence:

- company briefs
- comparison matrices
- product memos
- system diagrams
- PM case notes

## What A Hiring Manager Should Feel

If this curriculum works, the signal to a hiring manager should be:

- this person understands ads as a business, not just a feature
- this person understands our platform specifically
- this person can reason about tradeoffs
- this person can spot opportunities
- this person can onboard quickly into the domain

That is the real objective: domain fluency, company fluency, and evidence of strategic thinking.
