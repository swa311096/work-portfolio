# Private Equity

Operator lens on private equity. This section captures how I think about underwriting value creation, assessing risk, and understanding what actually changes inside portfolio companies.

## What I'm Trying To Build

My goal here is to build a working understanding of private equity so I can:

1. explain how private equity firms think about returns, risk, and value creation
2. deconstruct an investment case beyond the headline thesis
3. reason about diligence, operating improvement, and portfolio-company execution
4. think clearly about the tradeoffs between growth, margin, leverage, and exit outcomes
5. back that up with structured investment notes, diligence frameworks, and operating memos

## What That Means In Practice

There are four capabilities I want this section to demonstrate.

### 1. Explain A PE Model Clearly

For any PE situation, I want to be able to describe:

- how a sponsor makes money
- what makes a company attractive or unattractive
- how leverage changes the return profile
- where value creation is expected to come from
- what risks can break the thesis
- what a realistic exit path looks like

### 2. Deconstruct A Specific Investment Situation

For a software, services, consumer, education, or industrial business, I want to be able to answer:

- what the core thesis is
- what the real sources of growth are
- where margin expansion could come from
- what diligence questions matter most
- what the operating constraints are
- what the downside cases look like
- what management would need to execute well

### 3. Talk Like An Operator-Oriented Investor

I want to be comfortable reasoning through questions like:

- what is actually underwriting the return here?
- what makes revenue quality strong or weak?
- when is cost reduction smart versus value-destructive?
- what distinguishes a process problem from a market problem?
- what matters more in this case: growth, margin, working capital, or exit multiple?
- where would you focus in the first 100 days post-close?

### 4. Show Evidence, Not Just Familiarity

The point is to demonstrate judgment through clean frameworks and investment reasoning, not just surface-level knowledge of PE terminology.

## How I Structure The Work

### Layer A. Core Understanding

- PE fund model
- leverage and returns
- investment theses
- diligence workflows
- operating value creation
- portfolio governance
- exit paths
- sector and business model differences

### Layer B. Applied Company Understanding

For each target company or sector, I want to be able to articulate:

- business quality
- revenue quality
- margin structure
- operational bottlenecks
- diligence priorities
- value-creation levers
- exit logic

### Layer C. Evidence Creation

Every bucket should produce a concrete output artifact.

## What I Want To Produce

This section is designed to produce:

### 1. PE Knowledge Map

A master reference for:

- PE concepts and definitions
- return drivers
- capital structure basics
- diligence workstreams
- value-creation archetypes
- sector comparison frameworks

### 2. Investment Teardown Briefs

For target companies or sectors, I want short notes covering:

- what makes the business attractive
- what the investment thesis is
- what diligence questions matter most
- where the risks are
- what the operating plan could be
- what the likely exit story is

### 3. Value-Creation Memos

Short operator-style notes such as:

- how a services business could improve EBITDA without hurting quality
- where a software company has pricing and upsell whitespace
- how an education company could improve unit economics after acquisition

### 4. Diligence And Operating Walkthroughs

Short artifacts on problems like:

- how to assess revenue quality
- how to diagnose margin leakage
- how to prioritize a 100-day plan
- how to distinguish structural risk from execution risk

### 5. Portfolio-Style Interview Stories

I also want to translate operating experience into investor-relevant stories:

- growth opportunity sizing
- margin or cost structure improvements
- business model redesign
- GTM or monetization changes
- process and systems improvements
