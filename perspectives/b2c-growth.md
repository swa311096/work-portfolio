# B2C Growth

Growing consumer products at scale. This section captures how I think about demand loops, activation, retention, engagement, monetization, and the tradeoffs behind durable growth.

## What I'm Trying To Build

My goal here is to build a working understanding of growth so I can:

1. explain how a consumer product grows and where growth actually comes from
2. diagnose whether a problem is acquisition, activation, retention, engagement, or monetization
3. deconstruct a specific company's growth model and identify its biggest constraints
4. think about growth as a system, not just a list of hacks
5. back that up with structured teardown notes, frameworks, and product opportunity memos

## What That Means In Practice

There are four capabilities I want this section to demonstrate.

### 1. Explain A Growth Model Clearly

For any consumer product, I want to be able to describe:

- who the user is
- what job they hire the product for
- how acquisition works
- what activation looks like
- what drives repeat usage
- how engagement compounds into retention
- how monetization fits into the loop

### 2. Deconstruct A Specific Product's Growth System

For products like TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Duolingo, Spotify, WhatsApp, or Amazon, I want to be able to answer:

- what user intent drives visits
- what the activation moment is
- what creates habit or repeat behavior
- what the key retention drivers are
- which surfaces drive re-engagement
- what the main growth constraints are
- where the next growth opportunities likely sit

### 3. Talk Like A PM In Growth

I want to be comfortable reasoning through questions like:

- if top-of-funnel traffic is growing but retention is flat, what is actually broken?
- why can higher engagement still produce weak retention?
- what is the right tradeoff between notification volume and user fatigue?
- when does SEO create durable growth versus low-quality traffic?
- how do feed, search, and utility products create different growth loops?
- what metrics matter at each stage of the user journey?

### 4. Show Evidence, Not Just Opinions

The point is to produce artifacts that demonstrate structured thinking about growth, not just pattern recognition from reading case studies.
