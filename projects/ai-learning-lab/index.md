# AI Learning Lab

A structured guide to how language models actually work — written for someone who builds with AI but wants to understand what is actually happening under the hood.

Each chapter covers: the problem that existed, the approach that was tried, what it solved, and what it could not do.

---

### Before Transformers

| Chapter | What it covers |
|---|---|
| [Why Language Is Hard](01-why-language-is-hard/) | Ambiguity, implied meaning, context, exceptions |
| [Rule-Based NLP](02-rule-based-nlp/) | Hand-written rules, ELIZA, why rules don't scale |
| [Statistical NLP](03-statistical-nlp/) | Bag of Words, Naive Bayes, N-grams |
| [Word Embeddings](04-embeddings/) | Dense vectors, distributional hypothesis, Word2Vec |
| [How Neural Networks Learn](05-how-models-learn/) | Loss functions, gradient descent, backpropagation |
| [RNNs and LSTMs](06-rnns-and-lstms/) | Recurrent networks, hidden state, LSTM gates |
| [Encoder-Decoder and Attention](07-encoder-decoder-and-attention/) | Translation bottleneck, attention mechanism |
